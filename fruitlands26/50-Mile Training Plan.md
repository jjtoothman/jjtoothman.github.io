**Goal ride:** Sudbury → Harvard 50-mile loop · 51.3 mi · 2,200 ft elevation · target avg pace ~10 mph (~5h moving, plan 6h door-to-door)
**Target date:** Sunday, September 13, 2026
**Start date:** Sunday, May 17, 2026 (17-week build)
**Starting fitness baseline:** 10 mi outdoor, 8.54 mi / 30 min Peloton (today, 5/17)

## What you're already doing each week (the rest of your athletic life)
- **Mon night:** Full-court basketball, 1–2 hr — high-intensity, hard on legs
- **2–3x/week:** Strength training at the gym
- **Cycling:** This plan adds 2 outdoor + 2 Peloton rides (Peloton capped at 45 min)

Cycling has to slot around the rest of it without burying you. The biggest constraint isn't fitness — it's recovery. Basketball Monday means Tuesday is non-negotiably easy, and Friday strength training has to stay light (or upper-body only) to protect Saturday's long ride.

## How basketball + strength affect this

**Basketball Monday** is the biggest variable. A 90-minute full-court session is roughly equivalent to a hard interval workout — lots of stop/start, jumping, lateral movement, eccentric loading on the quads. You'll feel it Tuesday and possibly Wednesday.

- If Monday basketball runs long or hard, **shorten Wednesday's midweek ride by ~25%** rather than skipping it. Saddle time still matters; smashing your legs again does not.
- If you can't shake basketball legs by Thursday, **swap Thursday intervals for a second easy Peloton** that week.
- **Skip basketball entirely the week of the goal ride** (Week 17). One week off won't lose you anything; one tweaked ankle ends the project.

**Strength training** stays useful but has to fit around the long ride:
- **Tue (legs):** Squats, RDLs, split squats, calves, core. Keep it moderate — 3 sets of 6–10, leave 2 reps in the tank. You just spun on the Peloton; your legs are warm but tired.
- **Thu (upper):** Push/pull, core. Free of cycling concerns.
- **Fri (optional):** Whole-body conditioning *or rest*. If you're at 3 sessions/week and feeling cooked, this is the one to drop.
- **From Week 13 onward,** consider dropping legs day to once every other week. Heavy strength + 40+ mile rides = recovery problem.

## Climbing prep (the 2,200 ft matters)
The route has rolling terrain with the heaviest climbing between miles 18–26 (Stow into Harvard). Your current flat 10-mile loop won't prepare you for that.

- **Seek out hills on outdoor rides** starting Week 5. Add one notable climb to each long ride and grow from there.
- **Peloton climb classes on Thursdays.** Matt Wilpers and Christine D'Ercole have good ones. Set resistance honestly — don't cheat the climbs.
- **Learn to sit-and-spin uphill** rather than mash. Aim for cadence 70–80 rpm on climbs. Your knees will thank you at mile 40.

## Nutrition

Endurance cycling adds real fueling demands on top of basketball + strength. Four layers: daily nutrition, pre-ride, on-bike, and recovery. The biggest mistake people make on long rides isn't pacing — it's not eating early enough.

### Daily nutrition (Mon–Fri baseline)
- **Carbs are not the enemy** for an endurance athlete. Aim for ~3–5 g carbs per kg bodyweight per day during the build, bumping to ~5–7 g/kg on the day before a long ride. (A 180-lb athlete = ~82 kg, so roughly 250–400 g carbs/day baseline, 400–550 g pre-long-ride.)
- **Protein:** ~0.7–1 g per pound of bodyweight per day, spread across 3–4 meals. Critical for strength recovery and saddle-time tissue repair. A 180-lb person needs ~130–180 g/day.
- **Fats:** fill out the rest with whole-food fats — olive oil, nuts, avocado, fatty fish, eggs.
- **Hydration baseline:** roughly half your bodyweight in ounces of plain water per day. More on training days. If your morning pee isn't pale yellow, you're behind.
- **Don't undereat the day before a long ride.** Most mile-35 bonks start with a low-carb dinner the night before.

### Pre-workout fueling
| Workout | Timing | What to eat |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon basketball** | 60–90 min before | Carb-forward snack: toast + banana + nut butter, or oatmeal. |
| **Tue Peloton easy** | Whatever you'd normally eat | Low intensity, no special prep. |
| **Wed midweek outdoor** | 60 min before if last meal > 3 hr | Banana, granola bar, or toast with honey. |
| **Thu Peloton intervals** | 60–90 min before | Light carb snack + a little caffeine if you like. |
| **Sat long ride < 25 mi** | 60–90 min before | Bagel + peanut butter + banana + coffee. |
| **Sat long ride 25–40 mi** | 90 min before | Full breakfast: oatmeal + banana + nut butter + coffee. |
| **Sat long ride 40+ mi** | 2 hrs before | Add eggs to the above. ~600 calories total. |

### On-bike fueling (the rule that prevents bonking)
- **Under 60 min:** water only.
- **60–90 min:** water + electrolytes (Skratch, LMNT, Nuun, Gatorade Endurance).
- **90+ min:** start eating. **~200 calories per hour.** Bananas, gels, energy bars, fig newtons, PB&J halves — whatever sits well in your stomach.
- **Eat before you feel hungry.** Set a 30-min timer if you have to. By the time you feel hungry on a bike, you're already 30 minutes behind.
- **Hydration:** 1 bottle (24 oz) per hour as a starting point. More in heat.
- **For the 50-miler (~5 hr moving):** plan 800–1,000 calories on the bike, 2–3 bottles of fluid, and at least one stop with a real snack.
- **Caffeine** at the halfway point of a long ride (a caffeinated gel or half a Coke) genuinely lifts the back half. **Test in training, never on goal day.**

### Post-workout recovery
- **First 30 minutes (the "open window"):** 3:1 or 4:1 carbs to protein. Easiest: chocolate milk works. Or a protein shake with a banana. This is where most amateurs leave gains on the table.
- **Within 2 hours:** real meal. Carbs + protein. Rice bowls, pasta with chicken, sandwiches, eggs and toast.
- **Rehydrate:** weigh yourself before and after a long ride. Replace ~150% of lost weight in fluids over the next 4 hours (16 oz of fluid per pound lost × 1.5).
- **Don't skip protein after Tuesday's strength + ride combo.** The two together stack the recovery debt.

### Goal ride week (Sept 7–13)
- **Mon–Wed:** normal eating, normal hydration, normal sleep. Don't change anything.
- **Thursday:** start front-loading hydration. An extra bottle of water through the day. Skip alcohol.
- **Friday:** carb-forward but not gluttonous. One pasta meal at dinner. Continue hydrating. Lay out gear.
- **Saturday:** all-day carb day. Bagels, rice, pasta, fruit, smoothies. Hydrate steadily — don't chug. **No new food, no spicy adventures, no alcohol, no anything experimental.**
- **Sunday morning, 2.5 hr before start:** the big breakfast — oatmeal + banana + peanut butter + 2 eggs + coffee. ~600 cal.
- **Sunday morning, 30 min before start:** small top-off — banana or half a sports drink.
- **On the bike:** start fueling at 60 min, not when you feel like it. ~250 cal/hour. 1+ bottle/hour. Half a Coke at mile 25 or 30. Real food (PB&J, bar) at a stop around mile 30.
- **Post-ride:** chocolate milk in the car. Big real meal within 2 hours. Celebrate.

### Quick reference: what to actually buy
- **On-bike calories:** Clif bars, Honey Stinger gels/waffles, bananas, fig newtons, Skratch chews
- **Electrolytes:** LMNT, Skratch, Nuun (sample a few — taste matters when you're 3 hours in)
- **Recovery:** chocolate milk (single-serve cartons live in the fridge or cooler), protein powder
- **Pre-long-ride staples:** old-fashioned oats, ripe bananas, natural peanut butter, eggs, sourdough/bagels

## Substitutions and contingencies (when life happens)

### If one Peloton ride is 30 min instead of 45
Shorten **Tuesday**, not Thursday. Tuesday is just legs-flushing — 30 min does that job fine. Thursday is where you build threshold power on the bike, so protect that 45. If both end up at 30, you lose ~30 min of training per week. Over 17 weeks that's real but small — outdoor rides do the heavy lifting either way.

### If you miss Monday basketball
Basketball is your weekly hard-intensity workout. Best substitute:

- **Turn Tuesday's recovery Peloton into a 45-min Power Zone or interval class.** Legs are fresh; put them to use. Thursday Peloton stays as planned.
- **Alternative:** shift Thursday's intervals to Tuesday and do an easy spin Thursday.
- **Skipping the intensity entirely for one week is fine.** It won't dent the plan. Do *not* try to make up by stacking two hard days later in the week.

### If it rains Wednesday (midweek outdoor impossible)
In order of preference:

1. **Move the midweek ride to Friday.** Push strength to Thursday or skip Friday strength. Best option.
2. **45-min Peloton at endurance intensity (Zone 2).** Loses the saddle-time benefit but preserves the aerobic stress.
3. **Skip it.** A single missed midweek ride won't break the build as long as the Saturday long ride still happens.

### If it rains Saturday (long ride impossible)
Once the long ride exceeds 45 minutes, the Peloton can't replace it. Priority order:

1. **Move to Sunday.** Almost always possible and the right answer.
2. **If both weekend days are out and the long ride is ≤25 mi,** split it across two midweek days. Past 25 mi you need one continuous effort, so this doesn't work for later weeks.
3. **For 30+ mile rides that get fully washed out: slot back into the plan at next week's distance** rather than trying to make it up. Losing one week of distance build is recoverable, especially given the cutback weeks.
4. **Two Saturdays in a row washed out:** drop the next planned distance by ~25% and rebuild from there.

### If you travel
- **Hotel Peloton or any spin bike covers your two weekly Peloton slots.** 45 min × 2 protects the aerobic base.
- **Bring resistance bands** for hotel-room strength. 20 min beats nothing.
- **Don't try to make up missed long rides.** Slot back into the plan at the week you missed. Drop the next week's long ride by ~25% if you missed two weeks.
- **If you miss the Week 15 dress rehearsal,** push it to Week 16 and shorten the taper to one week.

## Gear and logistics to lock in before August
- **Bike fit** — get one if you haven't. Saddle pain ends more 50-milers than fitness does.
- **Padded bib shorts** (not just shorts).
- **Two water bottles + cages** on the bike.
- **Saddle bag** with spare tube, tire levers, mini-pump or CO2, multi-tool.
- **Know how to change a flat** before ride day. Practice once at home.
- **Bike computer or phone mount** with the Komoot route loaded. The route has 89 turns — you do not want to navigate by memory.
- **Lights** (front + rear) even for a daytime ride. Cars are cars.

## Ride day pacing strategy
- **Miles 0–10:** ride slower than you feel like. Save the legs.
- **Miles 10–25:** settle into your conversational Zone 2 pace. The Mass Central Rail Trail section (miles 5–10) is flat and fast — resist the urge to hammer.
- **Miles 18–26:** the climbing block. Sit, spin, eat at the top of each climb.
- **Miles 26–40:** the test of the day. Drink. Eat every 30 minutes. Get off the bike for 5 minutes around mile 30 if you need to.
- **Miles 40–51:** you will be tired. The route has another small climbing pinch near the end (mile 48ish). Knowing it's coming helps. Spin home.

## How to use this plan
- The Saturday long ride is the only workout you cannot skip without consequence.
- Don't chase missed mileage. Skip a workout > cram one in.
- Two consecutive weeks of feeling overcooked = take an extra cutback week. Better to arrive at September 13 fresh than fit-but-fried.
- If basketball, strength, and cycling all start fighting each other in August, **drop a strength session before you drop a ride.** Strength maintains; rides build.
- The Week 15 50-mile dress rehearsal is the most important workout on this plan. Treat it like the goal ride.

You're in great shape to do this. Basketball + strength is actually a strong foundation — you have leg power and aerobic capacity. The thing you're building is endurance and saddle tolerance, and the plan does that.
